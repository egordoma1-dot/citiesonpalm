AVAC — Automated Vacuum Collection System

cop:obj:us-ny-36061:avac · waste_network · geometry: approximate

Finding. Built in 1975 for four buildings. The island has since roughly doubled and the same vacuum backbone now serves twenty. No public record shows the system was ever evaluated for that load. A $1.7M upgrade was announced in 2019 as a six-month job; there is no completion notice seven years later.

Roosevelt Island Operating Corporation

cop:inst:us-ny-36061:rioc

Finding. A New York STATE corporation runs an island that is, for every other purpose — courts, elections, the post office — in New York CITY. Its board is appointed by the Governor. Nobody on Roosevelt Island elects it.

mandateTo plan, design, develop, operate and maintain Roosevelt Island.
[official_document] Wikipedia — Roosevelt Island Operating Corporation
legal_basisCreated by the New York State Legislature in 1984 (Chapter 899). Amended 2002 (Chapter 493), which moved the NYS Homes & Community Renewal Commissioner from chief executive to board chair.
[authoritative] NY State Archives — RIOC President/CEO files (agency history)
jurisdictionRoosevelt Island — but not exclusively. NYPD remains the primary police agency; DSNY, MTA, NYC DOT and NYC H+H all retain authority over parts of the island.
[official_document] Wikipedia — Roosevelt Island Operating Corporation
governing_documentThe 1969 General Development Plan, which accompanied the ground lease between New York City and New York State.
[official_document] New York Almanack — Roosevelt Island Opens New Aerial Tramway (Nov 2010)
fundingGround-lease rents from the island's residential developments, residential service fees, and transport/parking fees. Reported as operating without direct state or municipal subsidy.
