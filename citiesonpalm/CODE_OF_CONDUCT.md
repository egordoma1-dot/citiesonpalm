# Code of Conduct

## Standard

Adapted from the Contributor Covenant 2.1, with additions specific to this project.

We commit to a harassment-free environment regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression, experience level, education, socio-economic status, nationality, appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected

- Argue about the design, hard, in public. Disagreement is the mechanism, not the problem.
- Cite your sources. Assertions without evidence are noise.
- State which principle a rejection rests on. "I don't like it" is not a review.
- Assume good faith until it is no longer available to you.
- Accept that the answer to a well-argued proposal may still be no, on principle.

## Unacceptable

- Harassment, personal attacks, sexualised language or imagery, doxxing.
- **Using this project to target private individuals.** This is the one line whose crossing is permanent. Contributing, requesting, or advocating for records about natural persons outside their public office results in an immediate and irreversible ban. There is no warning step, no ladder, no appeal. The scope boundary in [PRINCIPLES.md § Privacy](PRINCIPLES.md#3-privacy-is-a-boundary-not-a-setting) is the reason this project can be trusted at all, and anyone testing it forfeits their standing here.
- Using project spaces for partisan campaigning. Publishing what a government did is our purpose. Telling people who to vote for is not, and it would destroy our ability to do the former.
- Submitting data obtained through a leak, hack, or breach. However true. However important.

## Enforcement

Report to **conduct@citiesonpalm.org**. Reports are handled by an enforcement group that excludes anyone named in the report.

| Step | Response |
|---|---|
| 1 | Private correction, written |
| 2 | Public warning |
| 3 | Temporary ban (30–180 days) |
| 4 | Permanent ban |

Escalation is not required. Severe violations start at step 4. The privacy-boundary violation above always starts at step 4.

Enforcement actions above step 2 are published in aggregate, annually, without identifying reporters.

## Scope

All project spaces: repositories, discussions, mailing lists, issue trackers, chat, events, and any space where an individual is representing the project.
